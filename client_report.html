Client Report

Android Navigator

When I was deciding the content for my website, it was quite easy, actually. Since my website just points users to different Android phones, the images used are just images of various phones and the text is descriptions of phones and explanations of how these phones work and feel and who they are for.

To communicate with my lead, I used Microsoft teams in a group chat with my lead and junior developers. We had no issues with communication. Everyone was communicative and responsive.

My developers did for the most part meet my expectations for my site. Although on the Archetypes page, the phones listed and recommended to the different Archetypes were supposed to be links to their descriptions on the Phones page, to make browsing easier. Other than that, it was great!

I think I'd make a more detailed document of my expectations and organize it for readability and priorities, that way the roadmap is clearer.

Lead Developer Report

Gusterpedia

I managed my team through another Microsoft teams group chat. My junior developer knew that they were responsible for the child pages, so I let them just go aheasd and make them and since it fit, I didn't have to intervene at all.

Our client wasn't super responsive, so my junior developer and I just went for it and hoped it met his expectations. I think we did well.

We used GitHub pages to publish the site and provided our client with the link. That way they could check in and look at it.

Using GitHub to manage the site was quite useful, actually. My junior developer and I were able to push and pull effectively and we avoided any sort of merging issues.

Junior Developer Report

Cartographer's Guide to Worldbuilding

I communicated, once again, through Microsoft teams. And there were no issues there.

I knew that I needed to make the child pages. So I went ahead and did them as best as I could, making sure to include accessibility and responsiveness in my approach.

I was certainly able to contribute to the site, with both child pages and their styling being done by me.

While using GitHub, we experienced no problems and everything went well working together with my lead digitally.

General Experience and Reflections

The part of this project that was the most challenging was hands-down just trying to position the content of the child pages I was responsible for. Margins, padding, and positions were a nightmare to overcome. Specifically on the Breath of the Wild sub-page, the Tears of the Kingdom div was especially difficult.

I most enjoyed being able to create creative solutions to the problems I faced and learning more about how to properly style something without a guide, just having to figure it out.

Honestly, I would've just been more organized as a client. I would have provided a better wireframe and clearer instructions. Other than that, I would not have changed anything.

I for sure feel more confident working on real-world projects, especially considering That I basically had to freehand these websites and just figure out exactly how to style everything without a guide or tutorial. That's the sort of stuff that translates to real life.
